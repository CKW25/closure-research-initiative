I. Research Program

The Closure Research Initiative develops a single premise: a mathematically closed physical theory must be written without external scaffolding. No background geometry, no pre-assumed state space, no external observers can be accepted as primitive. From binary comparison data alone, the admissible architecture is proven to be highly rigid—forcing quotient semantics, transport obstruction, curvature, and ultimately the geometry of a closed three-dimensional universe diffeomorphic to S³.

Chast K. Wolfe · v1 · June 2026
A finitary structural account of subsystem attribution in closed two-subsystem comparison worlds, using only binary comparison predicates with no topology, metric, or dynamics assumed. Rectangular completeness is shown equivalent to profile-maximality.

Closure Forces Spherical Geometry: Genuinely Closed Three-Dimensional Systems Are Diffeomorphic to S³
Chast K. Wolfe · v1 · June 2026
We prove that a genuinely closed three-dimensional system is necessarily diffeomorphic to S³. The closure constraint at the manifold stage forces the only consistent geometry, via the orthonormal frame bundle and admissible comparison groupoid.

Chast K. Wolfe · v1 · June 2026

A Factorization Criterion for Route Invariants with Fixed Endpoint Data
Chast K. Wolfe · v1 · June 2026
A factorization criterion for comparison data with fixed endpoints: a route invariant is determined by endpoint data if and only if it is constant on every endpoint fiber. Instances are recorded in quotient semantics, categories, gauge transport, holonomy, differential geometry, and general relativity.
