About

The Closure Research Initiative is an independent research program founded on a single premise: a mathematically closed physical theory must be written without external scaffolding. No background manifold, no pre-assumed state space, no external observers, no gauge convention, no causal structure, no field content can be accepted as primitive. Such structure is admissible only when it is forced by the requirement of closure under intrinsic comparison.

Starting from nothing but binary comparison predicates on a closed world—no topology, no metric, no dynamics assumed—the program develops the admissible architecture from this single constraint. Rectangular completeness defines when a two-subsystem world contains all product states, and is equivalent to profile-maximality. Quotient semantics governs how comparison data descends through identification, and the obstruction to subsystem attribution is characterized by the failure of rectangular completeness. Transport obstruction arises naturally from the comparison groupoid, forcing a curvature hierarchy. The orthonormal frame bundle of a genuinely closed three-dimensional system, together with the admissible comparison groupoid, entails that the base manifold is diffeomorphic to S³.

A factorization criterion for route invariants with fixed endpoint data records the same obstruction across quotient semantics, categories, gauge transport, holonomy, differential geometry, and general relativity. A four-axis taxonomy isolates the logical forms of primitive structural input that must be avoided: externalization, artificial factorization, premature globalization, and reification of repair. Structural closure—closure under intrinsic comparison—is logically distinct from cosmological closure (open vs. closed FLRW universes). The former is a precondition for invariant physical content; the latter is a specific geometric condition.

The program develops these results through the following lines:

  • Finitary comparison theory with binary predicates; rectangular completeness and the obstruction to product decomposition
  • Quotient descent, orbit semantics, and the classification of subsystem attribution
  • Transport obstruction from the admissible comparison groupoid and the induced curvature hierarchy
  • The S³ theorem: genuinely closed three-dimensional systems are diffeomorphic to S³
  • Factorization criteria for route invariants across gauge, differential geometry, and GR
  • Foundational taxonomy of externalization, artificial factorization, premature globalization, and reification of repair
  • Separation of structural and cosmological closure

The program is developed across seven research papers and a comprehensive monograph—Closed System Monograph—tracing the full arc from primitive comparison data through quotient semantics, transport obstruction, curvature, and the emergence of smooth geometry, field theory, scalar dynamics, and matter.
