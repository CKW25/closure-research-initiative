On the Relationship Between Structural and Cosmological Closure
June 2026
The mathematical sense of closure—closure under intrinsic comparison data—is logically distinct from the cosmological sense (open vs. closed universe in FLRW cosmology). The S³ theorem is a result about structural closure, not a cosmological claim—though it carries deep consequences for cosmology. This distinction took years to articulate cleanly; the preprint Structural Closure and the Cosmological Misnomer finally develops it in full.
What Closure Requires
June 2026
A genuinely closed physical theory cannot rely on external scaffolding. No background manifold, no pre-assumed state space, no external observers, no gauge convention, no causal structure, no field content can be accepted as primitive. Starting from nothing but the capacity for binary comparison, the admissible architecture follows necessarily from the premise. This is the central intuition; the monograph demonstrates that no other arrangement is consistent with the requirements of closure.
